# 🌌 Orrery: The Cosmos in Your Browser

https://meeseeks-mr.github.io/Orrery/

Welcome to **Orrery**! If you've ever found yourself staring at the sky wondering *why* the Moon looks like a chewed-up fingernail today, or exactly *how* Earth's wobbly tilt gives us both summer beach days and winter snowstorms, you're in the right place. 

This isn't just a spinning globe; it's an interactive, 3D educational playground that brings the intricate, high-stakes orbital dance of the Earth, Moon, and Sun right to your screen. No telescope required!

---

## 🎓 What Will You Learn? (The Educational Goodies)

Orrery is built from the ground up to make orbital mechanics intuitive, visual, and downright fun. It’s perfect for teachers, students, or anyone who wants to casually master the cosmos. 

Here’s what you can explore and learn:

### 1. Seasons & The Axis of Awesome
Ever struggle to explain why it's winter in Australia when it's summer in New York? Toggle the **Earth-Sun** module and turn on the **Seasons** and **Axes** layers. You'll instantly see Earth's 23.5° axial tilt. As you fast-forward through the year, you can watch the Sun's direct rays (the subsolar point) march up and down the tropics, physically demonstrating why the hemispheres experience opposite seasons.

### 2. Moon Phases, Demystified
Switch to the **Earth-Moon** module to finally understand why the Moon plays hide-and-seek. The app accurately tracks the Moon's synodic month, calculating its phase and illumination percentage in real-time. Turn on the **Shadows** layer and watch as the geometry of sunlight creates the waxing and waning phases we see from Earth. 

### 3. The Mechanics of Eclipses
Why don't we have a solar eclipse every month? Because the Moon's orbit is tilted! In the **Combined** module, turn on the **Moon Orbital Plane** and **Ecliptic Plane**. You'll see the 5° tilt of the Moon's orbit. The app even features an **Eclipse Alert** that triggers when the nodes align perfectly for a syzygy (yes, that's a real word), bringing the shadow cones directly across Earth or the Moon.

### 4. Tides & Gravity
Turn on the **Tides** and **Tidal Force Vectors** layers. You'll see beautiful, exaggerated cyan ocean bulges pulling toward (and away from) the Moon. Fast-forward the simulation and watch the Earth rotate *through* these tidal bulges, perfectly illustrating why coastal areas get two high tides a day.

### 5. Day, Night, and the Sky Dome
Jump into the **Sky Dome (Surface View)** camera mode. You are now standing on the surface of the Earth. Pick a latitude—say, the North Pole or the Equator—and watch the **Sun Path Arc**. You can literally see why the Sun never sets during an Arctic summer, or how the Sun's altitude changes throughout the year.

## 🕹️ Features at a Glance

- **Time Travel:** Pause, play, fast-forward, or reverse time. Watch a year fly by in seconds, or slow down to catch the exact moment of an equinox.
- **Dynamic Camera Modes:** 
  - *Free Camera:* Roam the cosmos like a caffeinated astronaut.
  - *Top-Down:* A god's-eye view of the orbital planes.
  - *Moon View:* Stand on the Moon's near side and gaze back at Earth.
  - *Sun View:* Look outward from the fiery center of the solar system.
  - *Cinematic:* Sit back, relax, and let the app orchestrate a beautiful auto-orbiting showcase.
- **Data-Rich Layers:** Toggle orbits, latitudes, months, perihelion/aphelion markers, and more. 

Dive in, mess with time, and don't worry—you can't break the universe here. Happy exploring! 🌠
